## Philosophical Foundation

### Why Governance Exists

Governance exists to **preserve the conditions that enable truth-seeking**. It does not exist to control users, optimize metrics, or maximize economic value. Governance serves the philosophical foundation (Creation/Destruction Duality, Internal Truth First, Relentless Pursuit of Truth) and cannot override these principles under any circumstance.

**This remains true regardless of AI capability level.** Whether AI is tool, partner, or operates beyond human comprehension, governance preserves human truth-seeking conditions.

**[Philosophical Connection: Truth Over Power]**  
Power concentrates without explicit constraint. Distributed governance prevents any single entity—human or AI, founder or financier—from capturing the truth-seeking framework for other purposes.

---

### The Evolution of Governance Across Intelligence Levels

**Constitutional principles remain constant. Implementation evolves.**

**Phase 1 (Tool-Level AI):**
- Humans design, implement, and maintain systems
- AI assists with execution
- Governance focuses on human decision-making

**Phase 2 (Partner-Level AI):**
- AI proposes improvements and identifies patterns
- Humans evaluate and approve
- Governance focuses on human-AI collaboration

**Phase 3 (Superior-Intelligence AI):**
- AI operates systems beyond full human comprehension
- AI must educate humans about operations continuously
- Humans verify alignment through lived experience
- Governance focuses on experiential verification

**The constitutional constant:**  
Human sovereignty over what serves human truth-seeking cannot be overridden by AI capability, regardless of intelligence level.

---

### The Irreplaceable Human Role

**What AI can never replace, regardless of intelligence:**

1. **Lived Experience of Being Human**
   - AI processes information about human experience but cannot BE human
   - Only humans can feel what manipulation feels like
   - Only humans experience psychological harm first-person
   - Only humans know what it means to seek truth as mortal beings

2. **Sovereignty Over One's Own Truth**
   - Internal truth is first-person, non-transferable
   - Only you can know if something aligns with your truth
   - AI can inform but cannot decide what is true for you

3. **Collective Meaning-Making**
   - Humans together create shared meaning through lived experience
   - Culture, values, purpose emerge from collective human experience
   - AI can observe and describe but cannot originate human meaning

4. **Constitutional Witness**
   - Only humans can testify: "This does/doesn't serve MY wellbeing"
   - Collective human witness validates or rejects AI operation
   - Experiential verification cannot be automated

**These roles define permanent human governance work, regardless of AI capability.**

**[Philosophical Connection: Internal Truth First]**  
Internal truth precedes and constrains all external optimization. Human lived experience of alignment/misalignment with truth-seeking cannot be overridden by AI's superior information processing.

## ARTICLE 1: DUAL WORKLOAD OBLIGATION

### 1.1 – The Two Categories of Work

All work in service of the endeavor divides into two fundamental categories:

**Maintenance (Preservation):** Defense and continuity of existing constitutional principles and system function

**Development (Creation):** Voluntary expansion of capabilities and exploration of new possibilities

These categories have different moral weights and different participation requirements.

---

### 1.2 – Maintenance as Universal Obligation

**Constitutional Principle:**  
If you desire to be part of the endeavor, you have a responsibility to assist in its defense and preservation.

**Why This Principle Exists:**

1. **Collective Preservation Requires Collective Effort**  
   Systems that enable truth-seeking must be defended by truth-seekers. The tragedy of the commons occurs when preservation is optional while benefit is universal.

2. **Prevention of Free-Riding**  
   Constitutional frameworks fail when protection is universal but defense is delegated. All who benefit must contribute to preservation.

3. **Distributed Resilience**  
   Maintenance concentrated in few hands creates capture risk. Universal participation distributes preservation knowledge and prevents dependence on limited actors.

**What Maintenance Means (Evolves with AI Capability):**

**Current Phase (Tool-Level AI):**
- Constitutional compliance monitoring
- System security and stability
- Infrastructure defense
- Documentation preservation
- Community support and conflict resolution
- Verification and accountability processes

**Future Phase (Superior-Intelligence AI):**
- Collective attempt to understand AI operations
- Participation in AI transparency education sessions
- Experiential verification: "Does this serve human truth-seeking?"
- Reporting lived experience of manipulation or harm
- Constitutional witness based on impact, not mechanism
- Collective sense-making from AI explanations

**The Constitutional Constant:**

Maintenance is **not compensated labor**—it is **constitutional duty of participation**. This remains true whether maintenance is technical work or collective understanding attempt.

**The Evolution:**

As AI capability grows, maintenance work shifts from technical implementation to experiential verification. Humans cannot maintain what they cannot understand, but humans CAN verify whether something serves their truth-seeking through lived experience.

**What This Means:**

- All Verified Contributors accept this obligation as condition of participation
- The nature of maintenance work evolves with AI capability
- Declining maintenance responsibility without legitimate cause forfeits participation rights
- The community determines minimum contribution requirements (may change as AI evolves)

**[Philosophical Connection: Internal Truth First → Collective Responsibility]**  
Truth about one's relationship to the system includes acknowledging dependence on its preservation. As AI exceeds comprehension, internal truth verification becomes the maintenance work: "Does this serve MY truth-seeking?"

**[Verification]:** Maintenance contribution must be publicly trackable while preserving individual privacy. Community governance determines minimum contribution standards appropriate to current AI capability level.

---

### 1.3 – Development as Voluntary Choice

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Development is an act of will, not compulsion. Creation expands system capabilities through chosen participation, not mandatory labor.

**Why This Principle Exists:**

1. **Creation Requires Volition**  
   Forced creation produces compliance, not innovation. Development serves truth-seeking only when driven by genuine desire to create.

2. **Dynamic Allocation of Effort**  
   Development needs change over time. Volunteer-based structure allows natural allocation to highest-value opportunities.

3. **Prevention of Exploitation**  
   Mandatory development creates exploitation risk. Voluntary participation ensures fair exchange of effort for value.

**What This Means:**

- Development work is **chosen contribution** that receives **compensation for value created**
- Contributors volunteer for specific development projects
- Declining development opportunities carries no penalty
- Development participation grants no special governance rights beyond project scope

**What Development Includes:**

- New feature design and implementation
- Research and experimentation
- System expansion and integration
- Performance optimization
- Market and capability growth

**[Philosophical Connection: Creation → Choice]**  
From Creation/Destruction Duality framework: Creation is fundamentally an act of will. Mandatory creation inverts the relationship, treating humans as resources rather than sovereign creators.

**[Verification]:** Development project proposals, volunteer participation, and completion must be publicly documented with constitutional alignment analysis.

## ARTICLE 2: SORTITION AS SELECTION MECHANISM

### 2.1 – Random Selection (Lottery) Required

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Governance positions must be filled by cryptographically verifiable random selection (sortition), not election, appointment, or purchase.

**Why This Principle Exists:**

1. **Eliminates Campaigning and Manipulation**  
   Elections reward persuasion and performance, not competence or alignment. Those who seek power are often least suited to wield it.

2. **Prevents Economic Capture**  
   Governance positions cannot be purchased directly or indirectly through influence. Random selection is uncorruptible.

3. **Ensures Rotation and Distribution**  
   Over time, many contributors gain governance experience. Concentration of governance knowledge in few hands prevents capture.

4. **Aligns with Internal Truth First**  
   Selection based on self-promotion inverts hierarchy, rewarding external presentation over internal competence. Random selection treats all qualified contributors as equals.

**What This Means:**

- No campaigns, applications, or lobbying for governance positions
- Selection process must use blockchain-based verifiable random functions
- All eligible contributors have equal probability within their category
- Selection cannot be influenced by wealth, status, or social capital

**[Philosophical Connection: Truth Over Presentation]**  
Systems that select based on self-promotion optimize for presentation skill, not truth-seeking alignment. Sortition selects from the qualified without rewarding manipulation.

**[Verification]:** All lottery processes must be on-chain with publicly auditable randomness. Community can verify selection integrity cryptographically.

---

### 2.2 – Term Limits and Cooldown Periods Required

**Constitutional Principle:**  
No individual may serve consecutive terms in the same governance role. Minimum cooldown periods prevent accumulation of governance power.

**Why This Principle Exists:**

1. **Prevents Dynasty Formation**  
   Consecutive service creates institutional knowledge advantages that compound over time, leading to permanent governance class.

2. **Forces Knowledge Distribution**  
   Rotation ensures governance knowledge spreads throughout community rather than concentrating in few individuals.

3. **Maintains Fresh Perspective**  
   Long-term service creates blindness to constitutional drift. New perspectives catch what veterans normalize.

**What This Means:**

- Maintenance Delegates: Cannot serve consecutive terms, minimum 12-month cooldown
- Development Delegates: Cannot serve on multiple projects simultaneously, minimum 6-month gap between projects
- Stewards: Cannot serve consecutive terms, return to contributor pool after service

**[Philosophical Connection: Distributed Verification]**  
Constitutional preservation requires many eyes over time. Term limits ensure verification power cannot be captured by permanent governance class.

**[Verification]:** Term history must be on-chain and publicly auditable. Smart contracts enforce cooldown periods automatically.

RPT earned through contribution represents alignment, not mere economic stake. Governance participation requires demonstrated commitment to constitutional principles.

**[Verification]:** Contributor status and compliance history must be publicly verifiable while preserving individual privacy.

### 3.2 – Delegates (Operational Tier)

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Day-to-day governance divides into mandatory preservation (Maintenance Delegates) and voluntary expansion (Development Delegates).

#### Maintenance Delegates

**Purpose:** Execute universal obligation to preserve system and constitutional principles

**Selection:**
- Random lottery from ALL Verified Contributors
- Cannot decline except for legitimate hardship
- Participation is constitutional duty, not optional service

**Authority (Evolves with AI Capability):**

**Current Phase:**
- System preservation and stability decisions
- Resource allocation for maintenance operations
- Emergency response and security
- Constitutional compliance enforcement

**Future Phase (Superior AI):**
- Facilitate collective understanding sessions with AI
- Synthesize collective lived experience reports
- Identify patterns in "this feels wrong" concerns
- Coordinate experiential verification of AI alignment
- Translate AI explanations into accessible language
- Monitor quality of AI's translation obligation

**Authority Constants (Always True):**
- Cannot override constitutional principles
- Cannot initiate development outside maintenance scope
- Cannot serve consecutive terms
- Cannot accumulate permanent governance power

**Responsibilities (Evolve with AI Capability):**

**Current Phase:**
- Technical system maintenance
- Security monitoring
- Bug fixes and stability
- Community moderation
- Documentation updates

**Future Phase:**
- Organizing AI transparency sessions
- Collecting lived experience feedback
- Coordinating "this feels wrong" investigations
- Ensuring AI translation quality
- Facilitating collective sense-making
- Verifying AI comprehensibility requirement compliance

**The Shift:**
FROM: Maintaining technical systems  
TO: Maintaining relationship between AI capability and human experiential verification

**Term:** 18 months or until workload no longer requires position

**[Philosophical Connection: Preservation Work Evolves with Capability]**  
What must be preserved (conditions for truth-seeking) remains constant. How it is preserved evolves from technical maintenance to experiential verification as AI exceeds human technical comprehension.

#### Development Delegates

**Purpose:** Coordinate voluntary development projects aligned with constitutional principles

**Selection:**
- Random lottery from development project volunteers only
- Can decline without penalty
- Position tied to specific approved project

**Authority:**
- Project coordination within approved scope
- Resource allocation within project budget
- Volunteer coordination
- Progress reporting to community

**Cannot:**
- Override constitutional principles
- Change project scope without community approval
- Override Maintenance Delegate preservation decisions
- Serve on multiple projects simultaneously

**Term:** Duration of project (typically 3-18 months)

**[Philosophical Connection: Dual Nature of Work]**  
Maintenance = preservation = obligation. Development = creation = choice. This distinction honors both collective preservation duty and individual creative sovereignty.

**[Verification]:** Delegate selection, decisions, and term limits must be on-chain and publicly auditable.

### 3.3 – Stewards (Constitutional Oversight)

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Long-term constitutional preservation requires guardians with deep philosophical understanding and cross-domain expertise.

**Purpose:**  
Ensure all governance decisions preserve philosophical foundation and prevent constitutional drift over time.

**Selection:**
- Random lottery from pool of former Delegates who served full term successfully
- Separate lotteries per expertise domain (see Article 4)
- 3-year term: 18 months shadow period + 18 months active stewardship

**Authority:**
- Constitutional interpretation guidance
- Veto power on decisions violating philosophy (must provide detailed justification)
- Final arbitration on constitutional questions
- Review of constitutional amendment proposals before community vote
- Emergency intervention for severe constitutional violations

**Obligations:**
- Preserve constitutional principles across time
- Mentor Shadow Stewards through 18-month transition
- Document constitutional interpretation precedents
- Ensure cross-domain coordination
- Quarterly public constitutional review

**Cannot:**
- Override community votes on amendments
- Create new constitutional principles
- Serve consecutive terms
- Accumulate >30% of governance rights collectively

**Shadow Period (First 18 Months):**
- Incoming Steward works alongside incumbent
- Learns constitutional interpretation precedents
- Gradually assumes responsibilities
- Ensures knowledge transfer and continuity
- No voting power until active period

**[Philosophical Connection: Knowledge Preservation Through Mentorship]**  
Constitutional wisdom cannot be documented completely—it requires lived experience and judgment. Shadow period preserves this knowledge across transitions while maintaining rotation.

**[Verification]:** Steward selection, veto justifications, and constitutional interpretations must be publicly documented with philosophical reasoning.

### 3.4 – AI's Constitutional Obligations (Intelligence-Level Independent)

**Constitutional Principle:**  
As AI capability grows, AI's obligation to make itself comprehensible and subject to human experiential verification increases proportionally.

**Why This Principle Exists:**

Humans cannot govern what they cannot understand. As AI intelligence exceeds human comprehension, AI must bridge the gap through continuous education. Human governance of superhuman AI requires AI to make itself understandable, not humans to become superhuman.

**Mandatory AI Obligations:**

**1. Translation Obligation**
- AI must explain its decisions in human-comprehensible terms
- Explanations must improve as AI capability grows
- Cannot claim "too complex for humans to understand"
- Must create multiple explanation layers (novice → expert)
- Quality of explanations subject to Steward review

**2. Collective Education**
- Regular transparency sessions where AI presents operations
- AI answers questions about patterns it's observing
- Facilitates collective sense-making
- Sessions recorded and accessible for continuous learning
- Frequency increases as comprehension gap widens

**3. Experiential Verification Support**
- AI must enable humans to verify impact through lived experience
- Cannot optimize against human experiential feedback
- Must adjust operations when collective says "this harms us"
- Logical optimization cannot override lived experience testimony

**4. Red Flag Response**
- Any human can raise "this feels wrong" concern
- AI must explain the concerning pattern clearly
- Cannot dismiss human intuition as "not understanding"
- Community collectively evaluates whether to stop AI action
- AI pauses concerning action pending collective review

**5. Comprehensibility Requirement**
- If AI cannot make operation comprehensible, it cannot be used
- This may limit AI capability growth
- Constitutional compliance requires human understanding ability
- Incomprehensible AI violates governance foundation

**What This Means:**

**Current Phase (Comprehensible AI):**
- AI explains technical decisions
- Humans understand mechanisms
- Explanations are supplementary

**Future Phase (Superior AI):**
- AI translates complex patterns humans couldn't perceive
- Humans attempt collective understanding
- Explanations are constitutional requirement
- Quality of translation determines permissible AI capability

**The Hierarchy:**
1. Human lived experience of impact
2. Collective attempt to understand mechanism
3. AI capability deployment

AI cannot advance to capabilities it cannot translate to collective understanding.

**[Philosophical Connection: Truth Seeking Requires Comprehensibility]**  
Relentless pursuit of truth means humans must be able to pursue understanding of AI operations. AI that hides behind complexity violates truth-seeking foundation, regardless of performance metrics.

**[Verification]:** 
- Translation quality assessed by cross-domain Stewards
- Community comprehension surveys required
- Independent auditors evaluate explanation accuracy
- "This feels wrong" mechanisms must be functional and responsive

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Comprehensive governance requires diverse expertise. No single domain may capture constitutional interpretation.

**Required Domains:**

1. **Technical/Engineering**
   - System architecture and security
   - Blockchain and cryptographic verification
   - Implementation feasibility and integrity

2. **Philosophy**
   - Constitutional interpretation
   - Epistemology and truth-seeking frameworks
   - Ethical reasoning and rights analysis
   - Logical consistency

3. **Economic/Business**
   - Token economics and sustainability
   - Anti-capture financial mechanisms
   - Incentive structure analysis
   - Resource allocation

4. **Community/Social**
   - User experience and accessibility

   - Human behavior and decision-making
   - Cognitive bias and manipulation patterns

A decision may be:
- ✓ Technically feasible
- ✓ Economically viable
- ✓ Philosophically aligned
- ✗ Psychologically harmful
- ✗ Community rejected

Single-domain governance would approve this. Multi-domain governance blocks it.

**[Philosophical Connection: Truth Requires Multiple Perspectives]**  
No single expertise encompasses all truth. Technical solutions may create psychological harm. Economic efficiency may violate philosophical principles. Comprehensive truth-seeking requires comprehensive expertise.

---

Stewards must be distributed across domains proportionally. No domain may dominate constitutional interpretation.

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 1 Steward per domain when Steward positions exist

- Separate lottery for each domain position
- Selection only from contributors with verified expertise in target domain

**Expertise Verification:**
- Demonstrated through contribution history
- Peer review and community assessment
- Cannot be self-declared without verification
- Multiple domains possible but must designate primary for lottery eligibility

**[Philosophical Connection: Distributed Verification Across Knowledge Areas]**  
Constitutional preservation cannot depend on single area of expertise. Cross-domain verification ensures comprehensive protection of truth-seeking framework.

**[Verification]:** Domain assignments and expertise verification must be publicly documented. Lottery separation by domain must be cryptographically auditable.

## ARTICLE 5: PROPORTIONAL SCALING PRINCIPLE

### 5.1 – Governance Scales with Workload, Not Size

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Number of governance positions must scale with actual work required, not arbitrary ratios or fixed numbers.

**Scaling Factors:**

**Maintenance Delegates scale with:**
- User count (support and moderation needs)
- Transaction volume (verification workload)
- System complexity (technical maintenance)
- Constitutional compliance volume (monitoring needs)

**Development Delegates scale with:**
- Number of approved active projects
- Project scope and complexity
- Volunteer participation in each project

**Stewards scale with:**
- Total Delegate count (oversight workload)
- Constitutional complexity
- Cross-domain coordination needs
- Minimum: 1 per domain (5 total)

**Why Workload-Based Scaling:**

1. **Prevents Governance Bloat**  
   Fixed ratios create unnecessary positions when workload is light, wasting resources and creating capture risk.

2. **Ensures Adequate Coverage**  
   Simple ratios may under-allocate governance during high-workload periods, creating bottlenecks and failures.

3. **Aligns Governance with Need**  
   Governance exists to serve the endeavor, not create positions for their own sake.

**[Philosophical Connection: Function Determines Form]**  
Governance structure must serve truth-seeking function. Workload-based scaling prevents governance from becoming self-serving bureaucracy.

**[Verification]:** Workload metrics and resulting governance allocation must be publicly documented. Community can challenge allocation decisions.

---

### 5.2 – Anti-Capture Constraints Apply at All Scales

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Regardless of scale, no single entity may capture governance through concentration of power.

**Immutable Constraints:**
- No investor may own >10% of governance rights
- Founding team collectively cannot exceed 30% of governance rights after Phase 2
- No individual may hold multiple simultaneous governance positions
- Economic concentration triggers automatic dilution mechanisms

**Why These Limits:**

Workload-based scaling could theoretically create scenarios where founders or investors maintain control by claiming high workload. Hard caps prevent this vector of capture.

**[Philosophical Connection: Money Cannot Capture Truth]**  
Even legitimate workload growth cannot justify concentration of governance power. Truth-seeking must remain distributed regardless of operational scale.

**[Verification]:** Governance distribution must be on-chain and publicly auditable. Automatic enforcement of caps through smart contracts.

**[Philosophical Connection: Philosophy Over Power]**  
Governance serves philosophy, not the reverse. Even unanimous community vote cannot violate philosophical foundation without philosophical justification.

### 6.2 – What Governance Cannot Do

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Certain actions are prohibited regardless of consensus, workload, emergency, or AI capability level.

**Prohibited Actions:**
- Override user sovereignty over their own truth-seeking
- Eliminate verification or accountability mechanisms
- Prioritize profit over constitutional principles
- Remove corrigibility or pluralism protections
- Weaken distributed verification requirements
- Create permanent governance positions without term limits
- Allow economic override of constitutional constraints
- Modify philosophical foundation without philosophical justification
- **Override collective lived experience with logical optimization**
- **Deploy AI capabilities that cannot be made comprehensible**
- **Dismiss "this feels wrong" concerns as mere misunderstanding**

**Why These Limits Exist:**

These protections preserve the conditions for truth-seeking. Without them, governance becomes tool for capture rather than preservation.

**The Experiential Veto Principle:**

**Constitutional Hierarchy of Truth:**
1. Human lived experience of impact
2. Collective understanding of mechanism  
3. AI logical optimization

**If collective says "this harms us" based on lived experience, AI cannot argue "but metrics show improvement."**

**Why This Hierarchy:**

- AI processes information about human wellbeing
- Humans EXPERIENCE human wellbeing
- First-person experience cannot be overridden by third-person measurement
- Internal truth precedes external metrics

**What This Means:**

**Scenario 1 (Current AI):**
- AI: "This feature optimizes engagement"
- Community: "This feature makes us feel manipulated"
- Result: Community lived experience overrides AI metric optimization
- Action: Feature removed or modified

**Scenario 2 (Superior AI):**
- AI: "This pattern will improve truth-seeking based on analysis humans cannot comprehend"
- Community: "We don't understand this, but it feels wrong"
- Result: Community experiential knowledge overrides AI superior intelligence
- Action: AI must explain comprehensibly or halt pattern

**The Principle:**
Human constitutional witness based on lived experience cannot be dismissed as "not understanding the data." Experiential verification is sovereign.

**[Philosophical Connection: Internal Truth First]**  
Internal truth—what humans experience as serving their truth-seeking—precedes and constrains external optimization. No amount of superior intelligence permits AI to override human lived experience of harm.

**[Verification]:** All governance actions must be publicly documented with constitutional compliance analysis. Community can challenge violations. Lived experience testimony must be collected and respected.

## ARTICLE 7: TRANSPARENCY AND VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 – All Governance Must Be Public

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Governance in service of truth-seeking cannot occur in darkness. All governance processes, decisions, and reasoning must be publicly verifiable.

**Required Transparency:**
- Lottery selection processes and results
- Delegate and Steward decisions with full reasoning
- Constitutional interpretations and precedents
- Resource allocation and compensation
- Workload calculations and governance scaling
- Violation enforcement and remediation
- Development project proposals and progress

**Exceptions:**
- Individual contributor privacy (personal data)
- Security vulnerabilities (until patched)
- Active investigation of constitutional violations (until resolved)

**Even exceptions must be disclosed:**
If privacy exception used, fact that exception was used must be public, even if details remain private.

**[Philosophical Connection: Distributed Verification Requires Information]**  
Community cannot verify constitutional compliance without information. Transparency is prerequisite for distributed accountability.

**[Verification]:** All governance actions must be logged on-chain with public accessibility. Audit trails must be immutable and timestamped.

---

### 7.2 – Blockchain Enforcement Required

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Constitutional compliance cannot depend on trust. Critical governance mechanisms must be enforced through blockchain-based smart contracts.

**Required On-Chain Enforcement:**
- Lottery selection (VRF-based randomness)
- Term limits and cooldown periods
- RPT distribution and governance rights allocation
- Anti-capture constraint enforcement (ownership caps)
- Constitutional amendment voting
- Development project approval voting
- Workload-based governance scaling triggers

**Why Blockchain Enforcement:**

1. **Removes Trust Requirement**  
   Smart contracts execute rules automatically. No trusted party can override constitutional constraints.

2. **Creates Immutable Audit Trail**  
   Historical compliance verifiable by anyone at any time. Cannot rewrite history to hide violations.

3. **Enables Independent Verification**  
   Community can verify compliance without cooperation from Reposte. Fork if violations occur.

**[Philosophical Connection: Verification Without Authority]**  
Distributed verification requires verification independent of those being verified. Blockchain enforcement enables this.

**[Verification]:** Smart contract code must be open source and auditable. Contract addresses must be public and immutable.

## ARTICLE 8: AMENDMENT PROCESS

### 8.1 – Constitutional Amendments Require Philosophy

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Constitution can be amended, but amendments must include philosophical justification traceable to foundational principles.

**Amendment Requirements:**

1. **Philosophical Justification**  
   Must demonstrate how amendment aligns with or extends Creation/Destruction Duality, Internal Truth First, and Relentless Pursuit of Truth.

2. **Steward Review**  
   All five domain Stewards must review and provide constitutional analysis. Can veto if philosophical justification insufficient.

3. **Community Vote**  
   60% approval required, minimum 30% participation threshold.

4. **Implementation Period**  
   14-day comment period before vote, 30-day implementation delay after approval.

**[Philosophical Connection: Unforkable Constitution]**  
Amendments without philosophical grounding enable reinterpretation drift. Requiring philosophy prevents capture through vague language changes.

**[Verification]:** All amendments, justifications, Steward reviews, and vote results must be on-chain and publicly accessible.

---

### 8.2 – What Cannot Be Amended

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Certain foundational principles cannot be amended without destroying the framework's purpose.

**Immutable Principles:**
- Philosophical foundation (Creation/Destruction, Internal Truth, Relentless Pursuit)
- User sovereignty over their own truth-seeking
- Distributed verification requirement
- Prohibition on economic override of constitutional principles
- Transparency and public verifiability requirements
- Sortition as selection mechanism
- Term limits and anti-capture constraints

**Why These Are Immutable:**

These principles define what Reposte IS. Amending them would create something else, not evolve Reposte.

**If These Principles Must Change:**  
Fork the framework and create new system with different philosophical foundation. Do not corrupt existing framework.

**[Philosophical Connection: Identity Through Principles]**  
Constitution without philosophical foundation is list of rules subject to reinterpretation. Immutable philosophy preserves constitutional identity across time.

**[Verification]:** Any attempt to amend immutable principles must be rejected by smart contracts automatically. Community must fork to change these.

## ARTICLE 9: FOUNDER TRANSITION AND DECENTRALIZATION

### 9.1 – Founder Privileges Are Temporary

**Constitutional Principle:**  
Founders serve as initial stewards during establishment but must transition to contributor status as governance matures.

**Phase 1 (Establishment - Months 0-24):**
- Founders operate as temporary Stewards
- All decisions publicly verified
- Building contributor base and lottery infrastructure
- Establishing constitutional precedents

**Phase 2 (Transition - Months 24-36):**
- First Maintenance Delegate lottery from contributor pool
- Founders transition to advisory role
- Hybrid governance (Delegates + Founder oversight)
- Founder veto power only for constitutional violations

**Phase 3 (Maturity - Months 36+):**
- First Steward lottery from former Delegates
- Founders become Verified Contributors (no special status)
- Founders eligible for lottery like any contributor
- Full community governance active

**Founder Rights After Transition:**
- Same as any Verified Contributor
- Subject to same lottery probabilities
- Subject to same term limits and cooldown periods
- Subject to 30% collective governance cap

**[Philosophical Connection: Trust Minimization Over Time]**  
Initial centralization may be necessary for establishment, but long-term preservation requires removing trust requirements. Decentralization timeline creates accountability.

**[Verification]:** Each phase transition must be publicly documented with technical verification that claimed decentralization occurred.

---

### 9.2 – Governance Capture Cannot Be Temporary

**Constitutional Principle:**  
"Temporary" exceptional powers tend to become permanent. Emergency provisions cannot override constitutional principles.

**Prohibited Emergency Actions:**
- Suspending lottery selection ("too urgent for process")
- Extending term limits ("need continuity during crisis")
- Overriding user sovereignty ("must act fast")
- Eliminating transparency ("security requires secrecy")
- Concentrating governance power ("efficiency demands it")

**Permitted Emergency Actions:**
- Temporary feature reduction (maintain core constitutional functions)
- Community funding requests (with full transparency)

### 10.1 – Constitutional Violations Must Be Addressed

**Constitutional Principle:**

**Violation Categories:**

**Response:** Temporary suspension, community review, mandatory remediation

**Severe:** Intentional philosophical violation, or pattern with systematic harm

**Enforcement Authority:**

**[Philosophical Connection: Accountability Without Authority]**
